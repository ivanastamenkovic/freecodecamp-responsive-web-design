Linkin Park

- a nu-metal band, which has significantly influenced the childhoods of countless fans around the world -

History

  • 1996 - Linkin Park is formed in Agoura Hills, California, by high school friends Mike Shinoda, Brad Delson, and Rob Bourdon, originally named Xero.
  • 1999 - The band changes their name to Hybrid Theory and records a self-titled EP. Later, they settle on the name Linkin Park.
  • 2000 - Linkin Park releases their debut album Hybrid Theory, becoming a massive commercial success and earning critical acclaim.
  • 2001 - Hybrid Theory receives a Grammy Award for Best Hard Rock Performance for their single Crawling.
  • 2003 - The band releases their second studio album, Meteora, which also achieves commercial success and features hits like Numb and Somewhere I Belong.
  • 2004 - Linkin Park collaborates with Jay-Z on the album Collision Course, blending their songs with Jay-Z's rap verses.
  • 2007 - The band releases their third studio album, Minutes to Midnight, showcasing a shift in their musical style and featuring the hit single What I've Done.
  • 2010 - A Thousand Suns, their fourth studio album, releases, exploring more experimental and conceptual themes.
  • 2012 - Linkin Park releases their fifth studio album, Living Things, incorporating electronic elements with their signature rock sound.
  • 2014 - The band releases their sixth studio album, The Hunting Party, returning to a heavier rock sound with guest appearances from various artists.
  • 2017 - The band releases One More Light, their seventh studio album, which features a more pop-oriented direction.
  • 2017 - Tragically, lead vocalist Chester Bennington passes away, leaving a profound impact on the band and fans worldwide.
  • 2018 - Following Chester Bennington's passing, the band members take time to grieve and reflect on their future. Mike Shinoda releases a solo album, Post Traumatic, which explores his emotions in the aftermath of Bennington's death.
  • 2020 - To celebrate the 20th anniversary of Hybrid Theory, Linkin Park releases a special edition of the album, featuring previously unreleased tracks, demos, and remixes.
  • 2021 - Despite not releasing new music as a group, Linkin Park continues to engage with their fanbase through social media, special events, and livestreams.
  • 2023 - The band releases Meteora 20th Anniversary Edition, celebrating another iconic album's milestone. The anniversary edition includes rare demos, remixes, and unreleased songs from that era.
  • 2024 - Linkin Park returns with From Zero, their first album since 2017, featuring new vocalist Emily Armstrong. The band is also embarking on a world tour, performing both new material and their classic hits.
